ABOUT

Paula Scher, aged 76, was born on the 6th of October 1948, in Washington, D.C., USA. Paula is known as one of the most influential designers, as she had a huge impact on the graphic design industry, especially in typography and branding. Scher has also been recognised as a "master conjurer of the instantly familiar." As her work blends pop culture with fine art, creating iconic, easy-to-understand images that have become part of everyday American life. You can see the passion Paula has for typography through her work, as she often uses large, expressive typefaces to create a visual impact.

Paula is known for having a unique, dynamic style, as she incorporates modern design principles with complex typography. She is also famous for transforming ordinary text into powerful visual statements, as her expressive typefaces help her work stand out from other designs. Scher is a personal favourite, as she mixes old styles with modern looks. As one of her many strengths is turning complex ideas into clear and engaging designs. Her work is more than just art.

FUN FACT

Paula created the famous Windows 8 logo. Her work on this logo was part of her role in creating the visual identity for Microsoft, and it helped shape the modern look of their brand. This project helped solidify her reputation as a leading designer in both corporate and tech industries.

NOISE FUNK

Paula was involved with Noise Funk, a design studio which she co-founded with designer Tibor Kalman in the late 80s. The studio was recognised for its experimental approach to design, as it mixed art with humour. The studio often pushed the boundaries of design, focusing on the idea as a form of communication. Schers style during this period of her career was influenced by the New York city scene, as it had an energetic vibe. The Noise Funk posters were important in shaping Paula's career as they marked a key change in her design style, helping her gain a reputation as an innovative and bold graphic designer
